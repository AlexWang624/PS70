Assignment: Kinetic Sculpture

Inspirations

I wanted to create a minimal, metallic kinetic sculpture as a very rough prototype for my final project. The visual inspirations for this assignment include kinetic sculptures by Anne Lilly, Jean Besson, and Pedro S. De Movellan:

I like the circles in these sculptures that create a mesmerizing motion, and I wanted to make a similar sculpture.


Materials:

1.Nema 17 42mm × 42mm Stepper Motor

2. 5mm to 8mm NEMA 17 Shaft Coupler

3. 1/16 Stainless Steel Wire Rope

4. 10 Inch Metal Loops

5. 3mm × 250mm Stainless Steel Solid Round Rods

6. A4988 Stepper Motor Driver Module

7. Arduino Uno

8. 12V 2A AC to DC Power Supply

9. 8mm × 290mm Metric Acme Lead Screw


Process:

1. Attach Metal Loops to Steel Round Rods and Lead Screw

My making process was very spontaneous. I did not draw a design plan and I started with experimenting with shapes and forms that were possible to make with the materials I had (being spontaneous was not a good idea because I had a lot of issues of keeping the sculpture balanace later in the process; I will never do this again).

The first problem that I encountered was: how to attach the loops to the rods and lead screw? I did not know how to 3D print customized components, and glue was not effective in terms of stickness and freedom of changes in shapes.

Then I thought about wire sculptures - maybe I could try to tie them toghether by wraping wires around them. And it worked pretty well! The hoops were attached to a central lead screw, with round rods as supporting structures.

And I had this first iteration of my sculpture:

However, then I found that I forgot to take down the motor mount plate that was left on the motor from the last time I used it. After I took the plate down, the structure became inbalance, and it kept falling to the side.

So I broke apart the sculpture and re-assembled it into a simpler structure:

It looks like a fast process now, but I actually spent an hour or two on building these structures. After I made the first iteration, I tried to wrap thinner wires between the horizontal loop and the vertical ones to create some visual illusion effects. Somehow I forgot to take photos for this process, and I got rid of the thinner wires after I noticed the imbalance issue from taking down the motor mount plate. So the whole process was much more chaotic than these photo documentaions.
